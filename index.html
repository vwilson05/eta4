Our Mission

Breaking down language barriers, building up futures

English Through Academics, Athletics and Arts Abroad

Since 2009, eta4 has been dedicated to providing free, high-quality English education to students in Vietnam. Our unique approach combines traditional academics with athletics and arts, creating an immersive and engaging learning environment.

From 2009 to 2019, our volunteer-driven 5-week summer camps taught over 14,000 students in cities like Hue, Da Lat, Bien Hoa, and Duc Linh (Phan Thiet Province) in Vietnam, as well as Taitung in Taiwan. After a pause due to COVID-19, we're excited to restart our programs in Summer 2026 in Hue.

Building Lasting Impact Through Local Empowerment

We know that 5 weeks is just a blip in time. The real, lasting impact happens on the ground every single day ‚Äî long after our international volunteers return home. That's why we focus heavily on recruiting, training, and empowering local volunteers in each community.

Our "teach to fish" mentality means we invest deeply in training local teachers and volunteers, providing them with the skills, materials, and confidence to continue English education year-round. We don't just teach students ‚Äî we build sustainable programs that live on through the dedication of passionate local educators who understand their communities best.

Announcement November 2025

Why We Paused... And Why We're Coming Back Stronger Than Ever

By Victor Wilson, Founder & Executive Director

When we wrapped up our 2019 summer program in Hue, we had no idea it would be our last for seven years.

What started as eta4 in 2009‚Äîa simple dream to teach English through Academics, Athletics, and Arts‚Äîhad grown beyond anything I imagined. We'd taught over 14,000 students across five cities in Vietnam and Taiwan. We'd trained hundreds of volunteers. We'd built lasting partnerships with local schools and communities. The program was thriving.

And then... life happened.

The Perfect Storm

In early 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic shut down international travel and gatherings worldwide. Suddenly, bringing international volunteers to teach in Vietnamese classrooms became impossible. Our Summer 2020 program was cancelled. Then 2021. Then 2022.

But if I'm being completely honest, COVID wasn't the only reason we paused.

During those same years, my wife and I welcomed our first child into the world. Becoming a parent changed everything‚Äîmy priorities, my time, my energy. Suddenly, spending summers away from my growing family felt impossible. The logistical challenges of coordinating programs from halfway around the world while caring for a newborn became overwhelming.

Life was pulling me in a different direction, and I had to listen.

The Guilt of Stepping Away

I won't lie‚Äîstepping away from eta4 was one of the hardest decisions I've ever made. Every summer that passed felt like letting down the students in Hue who were waiting for our program. Letting down the local volunteers who had built their teaching skills with us. Letting down the international volunteers who dreamed of making a difference.

But I also knew that trying to run the program at half-capacity‚Äîburned out, distracted, and unable to give it my full attention‚Äîwould do more harm than good. Our students and volunteers deserved better than that.

What We Learned During the Pause

The unexpected gift of these past five years has been perspective. Stepping back allowed me to reflect on what made eta4 truly special‚Äîand what we could do even better.

We realized the power of local leadership. Our Vietnam Program Coordinator, Linh (herself a former student from our 2013 program), kept the spirit of eta4 alive in Hue even while our international programs were on hold. She continued training local volunteers, maintaining relationships with schools, and proving that sustainable impact comes from empowering local teachers‚Äînot just flying in for five weeks each summer.

We learned the importance of rest and renewal. Burnout is real, especially in nonprofit work. Taking time to recharge‚Äîto be present with my family, to reflect on our mission, to plan thoughtfully rather than react constantly‚Äîhas made me a better leader.

We understood that pausing isn't quitting. Sometimes the most loving thing you can do for something you care about is to give it‚Äîand yourself‚Äîthe space to grow.

Why Summer 2026?

So why now? Why are we coming back?

Because we're ready. Really ready.

My daughter is now old enough that summer programs feel manageable again. The world has reopened. International travel is safer and easier. And most importantly‚Äîthe fire is back. I wake up excited about eta4 in a way I haven't felt in years.

But we're not just picking up where we left off. We're coming back smarter, more focused, and more committed to sustainable impact.

What's Different This Time

1. Local volunteers are at the center. Our 2026 program will be a true partnership between international and Vietnamese volunteers. We're investing heavily in training local teachers who can continue the work year-round‚Äînot just during our five-week summer program.

2. Smaller, more intentional programs. We're starting with just Hue in 2026‚Äîone location, 50 volunteers, and a laser focus on quality over quantity. We learned that trying to run programs in five cities at once spread us too thin. Deep impact in one community beats shallow presence in many.

3. Better support for volunteers. We've completely redesigned our volunteer training, mentorship, and support systems based on feedback from our veteran volunteers. Teaching abroad is challenging‚Äîwe want to set everyone up for success.

4. Radical transparency. We're committing to share everything‚Äîour financials, our challenges, our metrics, our failures, and our successes. You deserve to know exactly how your support makes a difference.

An Invitation

If you're reading this, you're part of the eta4 story. Maybe you volunteered with us in 2011. Maybe you donated to support a classroom. Maybe you're hearing about us for the first time today.

Wherever you are in the journey, I want to invite you to be part of our return.

We need volunteers for Summer 2026 in Hue. If you have five weeks, a passion for education, and the flexibility to spend your summer in Vietnam, we want you on our team.

We need donors to rebuild our program infrastructure, purchase new teaching materials, and support our local volunteer training initiative. Every dollar goes directly toward creating lasting impact.

We need cheerleaders to spread the word, follow our journey, and remind us why this work matters on the hard days.

The Best Is Yet to Come

Looking back at 14,250 students, I'm filled with gratitude for what eta4 has accomplished. But I'm even more excited about what's ahead.

The pandemic taught us that education is resilient. Communities are resilient. And with the right support, students everywhere can thrive‚Äîno matter what challenges the world throws at them.

We paused because we had to. We're returning because we're ready to do this work better than ever before.

Let's make Summer 2026 the beginning of eta4's next chapter‚Äîtogether.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about volunteering and donating

For Volunteers

No teaching experience is required! We provide comprehensive training before and during the program. All you need is enthusiasm, patience, and a passion for education.

Volunteers cover their own flights and personal expenses. We provide accommodation, training, and program materials. The total cost typically ranges from $1,500-$2,500 depending on your location.

The program runs for 5 weeks during summer, with an additional week for orientation and training. Total commitment is 6 weeks.

Our programs serve students from ages 8-18, divided into age-appropriate groups with varying English proficiency levels.

For Donors

Yes! eta4 is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. All donations are tax-deductible in the United States.

We publish annual reports detailing our financials and impact. 80% of donations go directly to programs, and you'll receive regular updates showing exactly how your contribution made a difference.
